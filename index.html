Projects

ROCKET PROPULSION LABORATORY

I joined Rocket Propulsion in October 2022 as part of their New Member Project (NMP). Engaging in NMP offered a hands-on initiation into rocket design, fostering collaboration and practical engineering skills. Guided by experienced mentors, I gained proficiency in CAD development and simulation setup using tools like OpenRocket and SOLIDWORKS. The program's inclusive atmosphere encouraged creativity, leading to the exciting launch of G-class model rockets at the Friends of Amateur Rocketry in Kern County, CA, solidifying my foundational knowledge and passion for rocketry.

More excitingly, after NMP I joined the Osiris Structures team. Osiris is RPL's new and first multi-stage rocket, meant to compete in the 2025 Spaceport America Cup, an intercollegiate rocket engineering competition. As part of the strucutres team I've conducted quantitative analysis of materials and geometric designs for Osiris's external structure with simulations. At our current pace we hope to finalize our design by the end of this year and with some in-house fiberglass layups. As the safety office of my team, I maintain a comprehensive understanding of our design's manufacturing methods and am knowledgable about safety protocols.

STANLEY 1913

Summer 2023 I was fortunate enough to be part of Stanley 1913's first intern cohort as a Design Engineer. This internship surpassed my expectations, offering a remarkably hands-on experience right from the start. It provided me with a thorough understanding of all facets of product engineering, including mechanism design, material selection, manufacturing methods, assembly, and performance validation. Utilizing SolidWorks, I created detailed 3D printed prototypes, optimizing design iterations based on material selection and manufacturing considerations. Additionally, I conducted research on metal forming techniques, contributing insights published in Stanley's internal documentation to improve manufacturing efficiency.

Based on my other projects it may be confusing to interpret how Stanley 1913 fits into my career path. My internship as a Design Engineer was driven by a desire for diverse industry experience and the challenge of immersing myself in a new setting. Despite being a consumer goods company, the engineering skills I acquired during this internship are versatile and applicable to any industry. This experience not only enriched my technical abilities but also honed my interpersonal skills. I thoroughly enjoyed my time in Seattle and working alongside such a dedicated and passionate team.
