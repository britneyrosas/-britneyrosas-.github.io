Projects

ROCKET PROPULSION LABORATORY

I joined Rocket Propulsion in October 2022 as part of their New Member Project (NMP). Engaging in NMP offered a hands-on initiation into rocket design, fostering collaboration and practical engineering skills. Guided by experienced mentors, I gained proficiency in CAD development and simulation setup using tools like OpenRocket and SOLIDWORKS. The program's inclusive atmosphere encouraged creativity, leading to the exciting launch of G-class model rockets at the Friends of Amateur Rocketry in CA, and solidifying my foundational knowledge and passion for rocketry.

< src="NMPTeam.jpg" style="width: 100%; max-width: 500px; height: auto;">

More excitingly, after NMP I joined the Osiris Structures team. Osiris is RPL's new and first multi-stage solid propellant rocket, meant to compete in the 2025 Spaceport America Cup, an intercollegiate rocket engineering competition. As part of the structres team I've conducted quantitative analysis of materials and geometric designs for Osiris's external structure with simulations. At our current pace we hope to finalize our design by the end of this year and with some in-house fiberglass layups. As the safety officer of my team, I maintain a comprehensive understanding of our design's manufacturing methods and am knowledgable about safety protocols.

Being a part of RPL has been incredibly rewarding. I consistently find myself facing new challenges that push me to learn concepts beyond my current skillset. What makes Osiris particularly meaningful to me is its embodiment of our shared ambition to soar to new heights, both literally and metaphorically. Osiris is my most long-term project I've committed to thus far, and I am eagerly looking forward to its launch date.
